The intention is to construct a simulated world to explore how social organization can arise as an emergent phenomenon, rather than as imposed structure. No predefined social roles, institutions, relationships, or concepts such as hierarchy, authority, or friendship would be encoded in advance. The system is meant to provide only the raw conditions from which such phenomena might arise if they prove useful for survival.
Perception is treated as raw data about the world, with relevance determined by consequences. Memory is conceived as a continuously updated predictive model, compressing experience into statistical regularities.
Decision-making is not meant to execute hardcoded rules, but to learn policies shaped by an optimization process rewarding survival and success. The guiding idea is that agents which predict better and act more effectively persist and propagate their characteristics, and that this combination of memory, learning, and selection allows strategies to emerge that were never explicitly programmed.
Communication is a special case of action and perception. Signals are meant to acquire significance through their observed consequences. Language should thus become an outcome of social complexity, coevolving with what structures it comes to coordinate.
Social structures are meant to arise from accumulated predictive relationships between agents rather than from labeled roles. An agent would not know that another is a leader, only that following its behavior tends to produce good outcomes. Leadership, alliance, hierarchy, reputation, and norms are all latent patterns of predictive relationship that became adaptive.
The guiding philosophy is that the simulation should expose pressures rather than provide answers. The intention is to shape conditions under which certain strategies become beneficial, while leaving the specific forms that complexity takes genuinely open. Emergence is treated as a discovery process, one in which the simulation reveals which patterns of organization are stable and adaptive given the conditions.
The long-term vision is a system capable of generating increasingly complex social organization through the cumulative effect of individual learning. If culture were to emerge it would be conceived as an emergent phenomenon the simulation provides substrate for.

The practical implementation is a grid-based artificial life simulation. Creatures move around a bounded grid that contains blocked cells, using A* pathfinding to travel toward targets their neural policy selects. Each tick, every living creature evaluates all active objects in the grid as potential targets, scoring each candidate through a small neural network that takes the creatures feature vector describing the candidate.
When a creature lands on a tile occupied by an object, it picks it up. When a creature lands on a tile occupied by another living creature, interaction can be chosen. Fights can kill creatures.
